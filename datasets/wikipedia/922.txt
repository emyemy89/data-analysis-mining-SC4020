It is the third largest German state by [[total area]] (after [[Bavaria]] and [[Lower Saxony]]) with a size of nearly 35,752 km and [[population]] (after [[North Rhine-Westphalia]] and Bavaria) with over 11 million people as of 2017. It shares [[Border|borders]] to the east with the state of Bavaria, to the north with the states of [[Hesse]] and [[Rhineland-Palatinate]], to the west with the [[country]] of [[France]] (along the river [[Rhine]]), and to the south with the countries of [[Switzerland]] and [[Austria]]. Its biggest cities are [[Stuttgart]], the [[Capital city|capital]], [[Mannheim]], and [[Karlsruhe]]. The [[Minister President]] is [[Winfried Kretschmann]] of the party ''[[Alliance '90/The Greens]]''.  In 2017, Baden-Wrttemberg ranked 2 on the [[Human Development Index]] (HDI) among all states in Germany.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Der HDI der deutschen Bundeslnder|url=https://weltbevoelkerung.info/HDI/deutschland.aspx|access-date=2020-12-31|website=weltbevoelkerung.info}}</ref>  == History == In 1952, the three states of [[Baden]], [[Wrttemberg-Baden]] and [[Wrttemberg-Hohenzollern]] joined together to found Baden-Wrttemberg.  == Politics == The Minister President is the chief of the [[government]] of Baden-Wrttemberg. The government is made up of ministers and state secretaries.  The CDU was the main political party in the state between 1953-2011, the Ministers President have all been members of the CDU. Between the years 1972 and 1992, the members of the Landtag of Baden-Wrttemberg were mostly members of the CDU. But the state is also a [[stronghold]] of the "[[Alliance '90/The Greens|Grnen]]" which were [[wikt:founder|founded]] in the early [[1980s]] in [[Karlsruhe]]. The [[election]] results of the Grnen in Baden-Wrttemberg have always been above the election average for the party in Germany. The highest [[mountain]] is the [[Feldberg]] (1492m). The [[Danube]], the [[Neckar]] and the [[Rhine]] are important [[river]]s which are in the state.  == Economy == Baden-Wrttemberg has the third largest economy in Germany after the economies of [[Economy of North Rhine-Westphalia|North Rhine-Westphalia]] and [[Economy of Bavaria|Bavaria]]. But the other parts of the state, especially the south, the majority are Roman Catholics.
