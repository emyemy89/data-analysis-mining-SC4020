Many things happened in the sixties, including the [[Space Race]], the [[Civil Rights Movement]], and the [[Vietnam War]].  The 1960s term also refers to an [[era]] more often called '''the Sixties'''. This was a set of [[culture|cultural]] and [[Politics|political]] trends around the globe. This "cultural decade" is loosely defined as beginning around 1963 and ending around 1974.<ref name="Barth84Exhaustion">Barth, John 1984. Intro to ''The Literature of Exhaustion'', in ''The Friday Book''.</ref><ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.nytimes.com/2007/11/05/books/05masl.html| work= [[The New York Times]] | title= Brokaw explores another turning point, the '60s  | date= 5 November 2007 | accessdate= 26 August 2011 | author = Maslin, Janet}}</ref>  The social revolution of the 1960s was part of a wider [[counterculture]]. Old ways were changed, new ways taken up. Typical was the introduction of the [[birth control pill]], and its effect on [[Sexual intercourse|sexual activity]], widespread use of certain [[Illegal drugs|drugs]] and a general disrespect for traditional ways.
