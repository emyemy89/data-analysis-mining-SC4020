<title>Governor General</title><text>'''Governor General''' is a representative of a [[head of state]].  The term is mostly used in [[Commonwealth of Nations|Commonwealth]] countries, where the governor general is a representative of the [[monarch]].  Sometimes the title is spelled as ''Governor General'', which is used in Canada,<ref>{{citeweb|title=Biography|url=http://www.gg.ca/gg/bio/index_e.asp|accessdate=2007-09-03|quote=Her Excellency the Right Honourable Michalle Jean, C.C., C.M.M., C.O.M., C.D., Governor General and Commander-in-Chief of Canada|archive-date=2009-02-25|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090225120559/http://www.gg.ca/gg/bio/index_e.asp|url-status=dead}}</ref> but sometimes it is spelled with a hyphen "''-''", ''Governor-General'', which is the spelling used in Australia.<ref>{{citeweb|title=Welcome|url=http://www.gg.gov.au/governorgeneral/|accessdate=2007-09-03|quote=Welcome to the official web site of the Governor-General of the Commonwealth of Australia|archive-date=2007-08-29|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070829022247/http://www.gg.gov.au/governorgeneral/|url-status=dead}}</ref>  == Other articles == *[[Governor-General of Australia]] *[[Governor General of Canada]]  == References == <references/>   {{politics-stub}}  [[Category:Governors-general| ]] [[Category:Government occupations]]</text>
