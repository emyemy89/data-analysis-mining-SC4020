At first, it was a [[dialect]] of [[German language|German]]  that Jews began to use in [[Europe]] about 1000 years ago. It was and still is used in the [[United States]], especially in [[New York]], and other countries that Jews have moved to.  Most of its words come from German, and Yiddish also has many words from [[Hebrew language|Hebrew]] and [[Slav]]ic languages, especially [[Polish language|Polish]], and some words from [[French language|French]], [[Hungarian language|Hungarian]] and [[Latin]]. Yiddish usually uses the Hebrew script.
