The Cable News Network's first newscast was anchored (hosted) by [[David Walker (journalist)|David Walker]] and his wife Lois Hart.<ref>CNN Press Room CNN's First Broadcast June 1, 1980 http://cnnpressroom.blogs.cnn.com/2011/06/01/cnns-first-broadcast-june-1-1980/</ref> In its first year CNN hired many political analysts, including [[Rowland Evans]] and [[Robert Novak]].<ref>The Telegraph May 16, 1981 On the Cable CNN is a 24&nbsp;hour video newspaper by Tony Seideman https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=2209&dat=19810516&id=P6krAAAAIBAJ&sjid=IP0FAAAAIBAJ&pg=4291,3826132</ref><ref>Cable News to Air Sunday Associated Press May 29, 1980 https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1454&dat=19800529&id=v8gsAAAAIBAJ&sjid=RhMEAAAAIBAJ&pg=7138,6800547</ref><ref>{{cite book| last1 = Turner| first1 = Ted| last2 = Burke| first2 = Bill| title = Call Me Ted| url = https://books.google.com/?id=O8bQBgj2znwC&pg=PA126| year = 2008| publisher = Grand Central Publishing| isbn = 978-0-446-54336-1| page = 126 }}</ref> On January 1, 1982 CNN launched a 24-hour sister newscast channel with no talk shows or commentary shows called [[HLN (TV channel)|CNN2]].<ref>Worth Noting August 21, 1981 St. Petersburg Times https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=888&dat=19810821&id=8Z4oAAAAIBAJ&sjid=OnsDAAAAIBAJ&pg=3119,5087088</ref> CNN [[broadcast]]s programs from its headquarters at the [[CNN Center]] in [[Atlanta]], or from the [[Time Warner Center]] in New York City, or from studios in [[Washington, D.C.]], and Los Angeles.
