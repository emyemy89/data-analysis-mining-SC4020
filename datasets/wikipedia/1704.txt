[[Captain (United States O-3)|Captain]] |unit          = [[First Motion Picture Unit|18th AAF Base Unit]] }} '''Ronald Wilson Reagan''' ({{IPAc-en||r||n|l|d|_||w||l|s|n|_||r|e||n}}; February 6, 1911  June 5, 2004) was an [[Americans|American]] actor and [[politician]]. He was the [[List of Presidents of the United States|40th]] [[President of the United States]] from January 20 1981 to January 20 1989. He was the [[List of Governors of California|33rd]] [[Governor of California]] from 1967 to 1975. Reagan was the 9th and 13th [[Screen Actors Guild#SAG Presidents|President of the Screen Actors Guild]], from 1947 to 1952 and again from 1959 to 1960.<ref name=biography/> Reagan was a movie, television and [[radio]] [[actor]] before he began his career in politics.<ref name=whitehouse/>  Reagan was born in [[Tampico, Illinois]].<ref name=whitehouse>{{citation|title=The White House|url=https://www.whitehouse.gov/about-the-white-house/presidents/ronald-reagan/|work=The White House|accessdate=January 25, 2010}}</ref> Reagan had a successful career in [[Hollywood]]. He was in [[Ronald Reagan filmography|fifty-three movies]].<ref name=whitehouse/> He married actress [[Jane Wyman]] in 1940. The couple divorced in 1949.<ref name=whitehouse/> They had three children. Reagan then married [[Nancy Davis]] in 1952.<ref name=whitehouse/> They had two children. Their marriage lasted until Reagan's death in 2004.<ref name=whitehouse/>  Before winning his presidental election in [[United States presidential election, 1980|1980]], Reagan ran for president two times in [[United States presidential election, 1968|1968]] and in [[United States presidential election, 1976|1976]]. He is [[United States presidential election, 1984|re-elected in 1984]] at the age of 73, making him the second oldest person elected president of the United States after [[Joe Biden]] who is [[United States presidential election, 2020|elected in 2020]] at the age of 77.<ref name=cnn>{{citation|title=Ronald Reagan dies at 93|url=http://www.cnn.com/2004/ALLPOLITICS/06/05/reagan.health/index.html|publisher=[[CNN]]|year=2004|accessdate=January 25, 2010}}</ref> He is known as the "Great Communicator" because he was a good public speaker. Reagan was also known as the "[[Teflon]] president" because any [[criticism]] or [[scandals]] against him never stuck or affected his popularity.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/news/opinion/editorials/2004-06-06-schroeder_x.htm|title=Nothing stuck to 'Teflon' president|last=Schroeder|first=Patricia |date=June 6, 2004|work=[[USA Today]]|accessdate=February 18, 2012}}</ref> Reagan still remains one of the most popular presidents in [[History of the United States|American history]] because of his [[optimism]] for the country.<ref name=biography/> Reagan was the first president of the United States to have been divorced.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.livescience.com/32658-how-many-presidents-have-been-divorced.html|title=How Many Presidents Have Been Divorced?|author=Stuart Fox|publisher=Live Science.com|date=June 18, 2010|accessdate=January 12, 2014}}</ref>  Reagan was [[Inauguration|inaugurated]] in January 1981.<ref name=biography/> As president, Reagan helped create a new political and [[economic]] idea. He created the [[Supply-side economics|supply-side economic]] policies. It was later called [[Reaganomics]].<ref name=biography/> Reagan's economic policy lowered tax rates. It created an economic growth and lowered [[inflation]].<ref name=biography/> In his first term he also survived [[Attempted assassination of Ronald Reagan|an assassination attempt]].<ref name=biography/> Reagan also declared a [[War on Drugs]].<ref name=whitehouse/> Reagan ordered an [[invasion of Grenada]] to end a Communist [[coup]].<ref name=whitehouse/>  He was re-elected in a [[landslide victory]] in 1984. During his second term, Reagan worked on ending the [[Cold War]].<ref name=biography/> He also ordered the [[1986 bombing of Libya]].<ref name=whitehouse/> In 1987, the Reagan administration faced a [[political scandal]]. It was the [[IranContra affair]].<ref name=whitehouse/> Reagan worked with [[General Secretary of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union|Soviet General Secretary]] [[Mikhail Gorbachev]] during his second term.<ref name=biography/> This led to the signing of the [[INF Treaty]].<ref name=whitehouse/> It decreased nuclear weapons in the United States and the Soviet Union. Reagan left office in January 1989.<ref name=whitehouse/>  Reagan was originally a [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]].  In 1962 he changed to the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] party.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://edition.cnn.com/2011/POLITICS/02/04/reagan.legacy/index.html |title=Reagan's myth has grown over time |first=Ed |last=Hornick |publisher=CNN |year=February 6, 2011 |accessdate=13 October 2014}}</ref> He is ranked high in [[Historical rankings of Presidents of the United States|presidential opinion polls]].<ref name="WWII">{{cite web |url = https://nypost.com/2014/07/02/obama-worst-president-since-wwii-poll/|title = Obama is worst president since WWII|first = Geoff|last = Earle|work = New York Post|year = July 2, 2014|accessdate = October 13, 2014}}</ref>  Reagan died on June 5, 2004 at his [[Bel Air, Los Angeles|Bel Air]], [[Los Angeles]] home from [[pneumonia]] after a ten-year battle with [[Alzheimer's disease]]. He was 93 years old.<ref name=cnn/>  == Early life ==  [[File:Photograph of Ronald Reagan (with "Dutch boy" haircut) Neil Reagan (brother) and Parents Jack and Nelle Reagan - NARA - 198605 (cropped).jpg|thumb|right|150px|Reagan as a toddler, 1914]] Reagan was born to [[Jack Reagan|Jack]] and [[Nelle Wilson Reagan|Nelle Reagan]] on February 6, 1911 in a small [[Birthplace of Ronald Reagan|apartment building]] in [[Tampico, Illinois|Tampico]], [[Illinois]].<ref name=cnn/> He had an older brother named [[Neil Reagan|Neil]]. His father was a [[Roman Catholicism|Roman Catholic]] of [[Ireland|Irish]] descent. His mother was a [[Protestant]] of [[England|English]] and [[Scotland|Scottish]] [[descent]].  The family moved to different places in Illinois when Reagan was a child. They moved to [[Monmouth, Illinois|Monmouth]], [[Galesburg, Illinois|Galesburg]], and [[Chicago]].<ref name=cnn/> His family finally settled in [[Dixon, Illinois|Dixon]], Illinois.<ref name=cnn/> They lived in a [[Ronald Reagan Boyhood Home|small house]] in Dixon. His family was very poor. Reagan did not have much as a child. Roosevelt]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/johnzogby/2011/02/23/reagan-fdr-share-one-thing-title-of-greatest/#180b71fc505d|title=Reagan and FDR Share One Thing: Title of Greatest|publisher=Forbes|accessdate=June 4, 2020|date=February 23, 2011|author=John Zogby}}</ref> Over time, Reagan became a conservative Republican. Even though Goldwater did not win the election, Reagan gained popularity from it.<ref name=biography/> In his speech, Reagan said,  ::<blockquote>You and I have a rendezvous with destiny. We will preserve for our children this, the last best hope of man on earth, or we will sentence them to take the first step into a thousand years of darkness.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.americanrhetoric.com/speeches/ronaldreaganatimeforchoosing.htm|title=Ronald Reagan -- A Time for Choosing|publisher=Americanhetoric.com|accessdate=October 6, 2014}}</ref></blockquote>  After Reagan gave this speech, many businesspeople thought that Reagan could run for [[Governor of California]].<ref name="gov"/>  ==Governor of California, 1967-75== After giving a speech of Barry Goldwater's presidential campaign in 1964, he was [[wikt:persuaded|persuaded]] to run for governor. Reagan ran as a Republican against the then governor, [[Pat Brown]] during the [[California gubernatorial election, 1966|1966 gubernatorial election]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.kcet.org/shows/lost-la/reagans-1966-gubernatorial-campaign-turns-50-california-conservatism-and-donald-trump|title=Reagan's 1966 Gubernatorial Campaign Turns 50: California, Conservatism, and Donald Trump|publisher=KCET|accessdate=June 4, 2020|date=August 19, 2016}}</ref> Reagan won the election with 3,742,913 (57.55%) of the vote while Brown won 2,749,174 (42.27%) of the vote.<ref>{{cite journal |last=Anderson |first=Totton J. |last2=Lee |first2=Eugene C. |date=June 1967 |title=The 1966 Election in California |url=https://www.jstor.org/stable/446081 |journal=[[The Western Political Quarterly]] |volume=20 |issue=2 |pages=535-554 |doi=10.2307/446081 |accessdate=February 6, 2017 }}</ref> Reagan was inaugurated on January 2, 1967.<ref name="gov"/> [[File:GOVREAGAN.jpg|thumb|right|180px|Governor-elect Reagan with wife [[Nancy Reagan|Nancy]] celebrating his election to governor in [[Los Angeles]] in 1968]] During his years as Governor, Reagan stopped hiring government workers. He did this to slow the growth of California's workforce. Reagan also approved tax increases to balance the state budget.<ref name="gov">{{cite web|url=http://governors.library.ca.gov/33-Reagan.html|title=Ronald Reagan|publisher=Governors.library.ca.gov|accessdate=January 10, 2014}}</ref> Reagan worked with the [[Democratic Party]] majority in the state [[legislature]] to help create a major reform of the [[welfare]] system in 1971.<ref name="welfare">{{cite web|url=http://ashbrook.org/publications/oped-hayward-99-gipper/|title=Welfare Reform: Another Win For The Gipper|publisher=Ashbrook.org|accessdate=January 10, 2014|date=December 1999|author=Steven Hayward}}</ref> The reform helped give money to the poor and increase the pay of the rich.
