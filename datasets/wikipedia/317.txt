SPACE.com |work=space.com |accessdate=5 August 2010 }}</ref>  The Milky Way has a [[diameter]] of about 170,000 or 200,000 [[light year]]s,<ref>{{cite press release |author=David Freeman |title=The Milky Way galaxy may be much bigger than we thought |url=https://www.nbcnews.com/mach/science/milky-way-galaxy-may-be-much-bigger-we-thought-ncna876966 |date=May 25, 2018 |publisher=[[CNBC]] |access-date=December 20, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180813182050/https://www.nbcnews.com/mach/science/milky-way-galaxy-may-be-much-bigger-we-thought-ncna876966 |archive-date=August 13, 2018 |url-status=live}}</ref> and is a [[barred spiral galaxy]]. The idea that the Milky Way is made of stars goes back to the [[Ancient Greek]] [[philosopher]] [[Democritus]].<ref name="space" />  The Milky Way has three main parts:  a ''disk'', where the Solar System is, a ''bulge'' at the core, and an outer ''halo'' all around it.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.damtp.cam.ac.uk/research/gr/public/gal_milky.html|title=Our own Galaxy - the Milky Way|publisher=University of Cambridge|date=1996|accessdate=2012-11-12|archive-date=2012-12-03|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121203114539/http://www.damtp.cam.ac.uk/research/gr/public/gal_milky.html|url-status=dead}}</ref> Although the word "disk" suggests it is flat, the Milky Way is actually not quite flat. It is slightly warped and twisted.<ref>Ghosh, Pallab 2019. Milky Way galaxy is warped and twisted, not flat. ''BBC News'' Science & Environment. [https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-us-canada-49182184]</ref>  This galaxy belongs to the [[Local Group]] of three large galaxies and over 50 smaller galaxies. The Milky Way is one of the largest galaxies in the group, second to the [[Andromeda Galaxy]].<ref name="seds" /> Its closest [[neighbour]] is the [[Canis Major Dwarf Galaxy]], which is about 25,000 light years away from the [[Earth]]. The Andromeda Galaxy is moving towards the Milky Way Galaxy, and will collide with it in about 3.75 billion years.<ref>NASA 2012. NASA's Hubble shows Milky Way is destined for head-on collision. [http://www.nasa.gov/mission_pages/hubble/science/milky-way-collide.html NASA - NASA's Hubble Shows Milky Way is Destined for Head-On Collision]</ref> The Andromeda Galaxy moves with a speed of about 1,800 [[kilometer|kilometres]] per minute.<ref name="space" />  ==Size== The stellar disk of the Milky Way Galaxy is approximately 200,000 [[light-year]]s (910<sup>17</sup>&nbsp;km) in diameter, and is considered to be, on average, about 1000 light years thick.<ref name="ask-astro">{{cite web | last = Christian | first = Eric | last2 = Safi-Harb | first2 = Samar | publisher=NASA: Ask an Astrophysicist | title = How large is the Milky Way? | url=http://imagine.gsfc.nasa.gov/docs/ask_astro/answers/980317b.html | accessdate = 2007-11-28 }}</ref>  It is estimated to contain at least 200&nbsp;billion stars<ref>{{cite news | first = Robert | last = Sanders | title=Milky Way Galaxy is warped and vibrating like a drum | publisher=UCBerkeley News | date=2006 | url=http://www.berkeley.edu/news/media/releases/2006/01/09_warp.shtml | accessdate=2006-05-24 }}</ref> and possibly up to 400&nbsp;billion stars.<ref>{{cite web | author=Frommert H; Kronberg C. | date = 2005 | url =http://www.seds.org/messier/more/mw.html | title =The Milky Way Galaxy | publisher =SEDS | accessdate = 2007-05-09 }}</ref> The figure depends on the number of very low-mass, or [[dwarf star]]s, which are hard to detect, especially more than 300 light years from our sun. Therefore, present estimates of the total number are uncertain. This can be compared to the one trillion (10<sup>12</sup>) stars of the neighbouring [[Andromeda Galaxy]].<ref>{{cite web | last = Young | first = Kelly | date = 2006 | url = https://www.newscientist.com/article/dn9282-andromeda-galaxy-hosts-a-trillion-stars.html | title = Andromeda galaxy hosts a trillion stars | publisher = NewScientist | accessdate = 2006-06-08 }}</ref>  The stellar disc of the Milky Way does not have a sharp edge, a radius beyond which there are no stars. Rather, the number of stars drops smoothly with distance from the centre of the Galaxy. Beyond a radius of about 40,000 light years, the number of stars drops much faster, for reasons that are not understood.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://arxiv.org/abs/0909.3857 |title=[0909.3857&#93; The structure of the outer galactic disc as revealed by IPHAS early A stars |publisher=Arxiv.org |date=2009|accessdate=2010-08-10}}</ref>  Extending beyond the stellar disk is a much thicker disk of gas. Recent observations indicate that the gaseous disk of the Milky Way has a thickness of around 12000 light yearstwice the previously accepted value.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.smh.com.au/milky-way-fatter-than-first-thought/20080220-1tbv.html |title=Milky Way fatter than first thought |accessdate=2008-04-24 |date=2008 |work=The Sydney Morning Herald |publisher=Australian Associated Press }}</ref> As a guide to the relative physical scale of the Milky Way, if the Solar System out to the orbit of [[Pluto]] were reduced to the size of a [[quarter (U.S. coin)|US quarter]] (about an inch or 25&nbsp;mm in diameter), the Milky Way would have a diameter of 2,000&nbsp;kilometers.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.cfa.harvard.edu/seuforum/howfar/across.html | work= NASA-Smithsonian Education Forum on the Structure and Evolution of the Universe, at the Harvard Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics  | accessdate=March 13, 2013 | title=How Big is Our Universe: How far is it across the Milky Way?}}</ref> At 220&nbsp;kilometers per second it takes the [[Solar System]] about 240 million years to complete one orbit of the Galaxy (a galactic year).<ref>{{cite book| author = Linda S. Sparke| author2 = John S. Gallagher, III| title = Galaxies in the Universe: An Introduction| url = https://archive.org/details/galaxiesuniverse00spar_886| year = 2007| publisher = Cambridge University Press| isbn = 1-139-46238-5| page = [https://archive.org/details/galaxiesuniverse00spar_886/page/n101 90] }}</ref>  The [[Galactic halo]] extends outward, but is limited in size by the orbits of two Milky Way satellites, the Large and the Small [[Magellanic Clouds]], whose [[Apsis|closest approach]] is at about 180,000 light years.<ref>{{cite journal | first = | display-authors = etal | last = Connors | title=N-body simulations of the Magellanic stream | journal=Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society | year=2007 | url=http://cdsads.u-strasbg.fr/cgi-bin/nph-bib_query?bibcode=2006MNRAS.371..108C&amp;db_key=AST&amp;data_type=HTML&amp;format=&amp;high=45a3b56f2301128 | accessdate=2007-01-26 | doi=10.1111/j.1365-2966.2006.10659.x | volume = 371 | page = 108 | last2 = Kawata | first2 = Daisuke | last3 = Gibson | first3 = Brad K. }}</ref> At this distance or beyond, the orbits of most halo objects would be disrupted by the Magellanic Clouds, and the objects would likely be ejected from the vicinity of the Milky Way.  == Galactic center == [[File:Milky Way Spiral Arm.svg|230px|right|thumb|Observed spiral structure of the Milky Way galaxy.<ref>Taylor J.H. & Cordes J.M 1993.
