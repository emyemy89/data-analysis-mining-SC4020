commissions|reconstituted]] on 8 June 1983.}} }} }} '''Anthony Charles Lynton''' "'''Tony'''" '''Blair''' (born 6 May 1953) is a British politician who served as [[Prime Minister of the United Kingdom]] from 1997 to 2007. He was [[Leader of the Labour Party (UK)|Leader]] of the [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour Party]] from 1994 to 2007 and [[Member of Parliament (United Kingdom)|Member of Parliament]] (MP) for [[Sedgefield (UK Parliament constituency)|Sedgefield]] from 1983 to 2007. On the day he stood down as Prime Minister and MP, he was made official [[diplomat|Envoy]] of the [[Quartet on the Middle East|Quartet]] on the [[Middle East]] on behalf of the [[United Nations]], the [[European Union]], the United States and Russia.<ref name="envoy">{{cite news|title=Blair becomes Middle East envoy |publisher=BBC |work=BBC News |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/6244358.stm |date=27 June 2007 |accessdate=2007-06-27}}</ref>  Blair was elected Leader of the Labour Party in the [[1994 Labour Party leadership election|1994 leadership election]] following the sudden death of his predecessor, [[John Smith (Labour Party leader)|John Smith]]. Under Blair's leadership the party abandoned many policies it had held for decades. Labour won a [[landslide victory]] in the [[1997 United Kingdom general election|1997 general election]], which ended 18 years of [[Conservative Party (UK)|Conservative]] rule with the heaviest Conservative defeat since [[1832 United Kingdom general election|1832]].<ref>{{cite book|first=John|last=Kingdom|title=Government and Politics in Britain: An Introduction|publisher=Polity Press|edition=3rd edition|date=April 2003|pages=299|isbn=978-0745625942}}</ref>  He was the Labour Party's longest-serving Prime Minister and the only leader to have taken the party to three uninterrupted [[general election]] victories.   [[Gordon Brown]], [[Chancellor of the Exchequer]] during all Blair's ten years in office, succeeded him as party leader and Prime Minister in 2007.<ref>{{cite news |title=Brown is UK's new prime minister |publisher=BBC |work=BBC News |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/6245682.stm |date=27 June 2007 |accessdate=2007-06-27}}</ref>  ==Early life== Blair was born at Queen Mary Maternity Home in [[Edinburgh]] on 6 May 1953. He was the second son of Leo and Hazel Blair. Blair joined the Labour Party in 1975.  ==Career== Blair was a lawyer before becoming a politician. He married [[Cherie Blair|Cherie Booth]], also a [[lawyer]], who graduated from the [[London School of Economics]] with a first-class honours [[degree]]. Blair himself left [[Oxford University]] with a second-class degree. They have four children: Euan, Nicky, Kathryn, and Leo. There was a controversy over Blair sending Euan to a [[grant-maintained school]]. As a result of this, [[Alastair Campbell]] discovered Blair "standing stark naked reading the ''[[Daily Mail]]''."<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.timesonline.co.uk/tol/news/politics/the_blair_years/article2059918.ece|title=The Best Bits in the Blair Years|work=TimesOnline|accessdate=5 December 2009|archive-date=21 August 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080821204809/http://timesonline.co.uk/tol/news/politics/the_blair_years/article2059918.ece|url-status=dead}}</ref>  ===As Prime Minister=== As [[Leader of the Labour Party (UK)|Leader of the Labour Party]], he won the [[1997 United Kingdom general election|1997]], [[2001 United Kingdom general election|2001]] and [[2005 United Kingdom general election|2005]] UK general elections. Blair is the first and only Labour Party leader to have won three general elections in a row.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.labour.org.uk/historyofthelabourparty3|title=History of the Labour Party|website=Labour.org.uk|access-date=2014-09-02|archive-date=2012-02-09|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120209073823/http://www.labour.org.uk/historyofthelabourparty3|url-status=dead}}</ref>  He attributes his success in politics to a pair of "lucky [[Brogue shoe|brogue]]s", which he wore for every single [[Prime Minister's Questions]] of his leadership. He claimed that "cheap shoes are a false economy".<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.timesonline.co.uk/tol/news/politics/the_blair_years/article1969242.ece|title=The Secret of His Success? A Pair of Lucky Brogues |work=TimesOnline|accessdate=5 December 2009}}{{dead link|date=February 2020}}</ref>  ===After-PM=== On the day of his resignation, Blair was made an official [[envoy]] in the [[Middle East]].
