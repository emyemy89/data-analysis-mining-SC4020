The [[capital city]] is [[Tallinn]]. Estonia's neighbors are [[Sweden]], [[Finland]], [[Russia]] and [[Latvia]]. Its [[population]] is 1,332,893.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.google.com/publicdata/explore?ds=wb-wdi&met=sp_pop_totl&idim=country:EST&dl=en&hl=en&q=population+of+estonia|title=World Bank, World Development Indicators - Google Public Data Explorer|website=www.google.com}}</ref>  The territory of Estonia has the mainland and 2,222 islands in the [[Baltic Sea]].<ref>Matthew Holehouse [https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/worldnews/europe/estonia/11830643/Estonia-discovers-800-new-islands.html Estonia discovers it's actually larger after finding 800 new islands] ''The Telegraph'', 28 August 2015</ref>  ==History== People have lived in Estonia since at least 9,000&nbsp;BC. The oldest known settlement in Estonia is the [[Pulli settlement]], which was on the banks of the [[Prnu River]].  Estonia became [[Treaty of Tartu (RussianEstonian)|independent from Russia]] in 1920 after a brief [[Estonian War of Independence|War of Independence]] at the end of [[World War I]]. During [[World War II]] (19391945), Estonia was occupied by the [[Soviet Union]] and [[Nazi Germany|Germany]]. It then became the [[Estonian Soviet Socialist Republic]] and lost its independence.  Estonia had its independence restored in 1991.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.estonica.org/en/The_August_coup_and_Estonian_independence_1991/|title=The August coup and Estonian independence (1991)|website=Estonica.org}}</ref> It became a member of the [[North Atlantic Treaty Organization]] (NATO) on 29 March 2004. It joined the [[European Union]] on 1 May 2004.  In January 2021, Estonia became the only country in the world led by women by having a female [[President of Estonia|president]] ([[Kersti Kaljulaid]]) and female [[Prime Minister of Estonia|prime minister]] ([[Kaja Kallas]]).<ref>{{cite web |last1=Hankewitz |first1=Sten |title=Estonia becomes the only country in the world led by women |url=https://estonianworld.com/life/estonia-becomes-the-only-country-in-the-world-led-by-women/ |website=Estonian World |access-date=26 January 2021 |date=26 January 2021}}</ref>  ==Geography== Estonia is bordered to the north by the [[Gulf of Finland]] with [[Finland]] on the other side, to the west by the [[Baltic Sea]] with [[Sweden]] on the other side, to the south by [[Latvia]] (343&nbsp;km), and to the east by [[Lake Peipus]] and [[Russia]] (338.6&nbsp;km).<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.eesti.ee/eng/riik/eesti_vabariik/ |title= Estonian Republic |accessdate= 21 July 2011 |url-status= unfit | archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20110721204649/http://www.eesti.ee/eng/riik/eesti_vabariik/ |archivedate= 21 July 2011}}. Official website of the Republic of Estonia (in Estonian)</ref>  Average elevation reaches only {{convert|50|m|ft|0}}. The country's highest point is the [[Suur Munamgi]] in the southeast at {{convert|318|m|ft|0}}.  Estonia has many [[forest]]s, almost 50% of the land is covered with [[forest]]s.<ref name="timber">[http://www.estoniantimber.ee/facts/ Facts] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170202081812/http://www.estoniantimber.ee/facts/|date=2 February 2017}} Estonian Timber</ref> The most common tree species are pine, spruce and birch.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://europa.eu/rapid/press-release_STAT-13-154_en.htm|title=European Commission  PRESS RELEASES  Press release  Land Use/Cover Area frame Survey 2012 Buildings, roads and other artificial areas cover 5% of the EU ...and forests 40%|accessdate=27 March 2015}}</ref> Estonia has over 1,400 [[lakes]] and over 2,000 [[island]]s. The longest rivers are [[Vhandu]] ({{convert|162|km|mi|0|abbr=on|disp=or}}), [[Prnu (river)|Prnu]] ({{convert|144|km|mi|0|abbr=on|disp=or}}), and [[Pltsamaa (river)|Pltsamaa]] ({{convert|135|km|mi|0|abbr=on|disp=or}}).  ==Biodiversity== Mammals in Estonia include the [[grey wolf]], [[Eurasian lynx|lynx]], [[brown bear]], [[red fox]], [[European badger|badger]], [[wild boar]], [[moose]], [[red deer]], [[roe deer]], [[European beaver|beaver]], [[Eurasian otter|otter]], [[grey seal]], and [[ringed seal]]. The [[Critically endangered species|critically endangered]] [[European mink]] has been successfully reintroduced to the island of [[Hiiumaa]]. The rare [[Siberian flying squirrel]] is present in east Estonia.<ref>{{cite book| last1 = Taylor| first1 = Neil| title = Estonia| year = 2014| publisher = Bradt Travel Guides| isbn = 978-1-84162-487-7| pages = 67 }}</ref><ref>{{cite book| last1 = Taylor| first1 = Neil| title = Estonia| year = 2014| publisher = Bradt Travel Guides| isbn = 978-1-84162-487-7| page = 4 }}</ref>  Over 300 bird species have been found in Estonia. These include the [[white-tailed eagle]], [[lesser spotted eagle]], [[golden eagle]], [[western capercaillie]], [[Black Stork|black]] and [[white stork]], many species of [[owls]], [[wader]]s, [[geese]] and many others.<ref>{{cite book| last1 = Taylor| first1 = Neil| title = Estonia| year = 2014| publisher = Bradt Travel Guides| isbn = 978-1-84162-487-7| pages = 78 }}</ref> The [[Barn swallow]] is the [[national bird]] of Estonia.<ref>{{cite book| last1 = Spilling| first1 = Michael| title = Estonia| year = 2010| publisher = Marshall Cavendish| isbn = 978-1-84162-487-7| page = 11 }}</ref>  [[Protected areas of Estonia|Protected areas]] cover 18% of Estonian land and 26% of its sea territory. There are 5 national parks, 159 nature reserves, and many other protection areas.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.keskkonnaamet.ee/en/news/nature-conservation-estonia |title=Nature conservation in Estonia |publisher=Estonian Environmental Board |date=16 November 2017 |accessdate=23 February 2018}}</ref>  ==Government== The [[Riigikogu]] is the name of the parliament in Estonia.  ===Administrative regions=== [[File:Estonian administrative divisions 2017 with labels.svg|thumb|upright=1.4|Administrative divisions of Estonia]] Since administrative reform in 2017, there are in total 79 local governments. This includes 15 towns and 64 rural [[municipalities]]. In 2002, Estonia hosted the event.  The Estonian [[National Day]] is the [[Independence Day (Estonia)|Independence Day]] celebrated on 24 February.
