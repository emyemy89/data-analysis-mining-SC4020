<title>Colour</title><text>[[File:AdditiveColorMixiing.svg|thumb|Results of adding different colours of light]] [[File:SubtractiveColorMixing.png|thumb|Results of subtracting different colours of light]] [[File:Pigment Colours - Classification.jpg|thumb|Pigment Colours - Classification]] '''Colour''' or '''color'''<ref>See [[WP:MOS#National varieties of English]].</ref> is a [[property]] of [[light]] as [[Vision|seen]] by people. In Commonwealth English, it is spelled colour (notice the U), while in American English it is color (no U).  The most common colour names are:  * [[Red]] * [[Orange (color)|Orange]] * [[Yellow]] * [[Green]] * [[Blue]] *[[Cyan]] * [[Purple]] *[[White]] *[[Black]] * [[Brown]] * [[Magenta]] * [[Tan (color)|Tan]] * [[Olive (color)|Olive]] * [[Maroon (color)|Maroon]] * [[Navy blue|Navy]] * [[Aquamarine (color)|Aquamarine]] * [[Turquoise (color)|Turquoise]] * [[Silver (color)|Silver]] * [[Lime (color)|Lime]] * [[Teal (color)|Teal]] * [[Indigo]] * [[Violet (color)|Violet]] * [[Pink]] * [[Gray]] "[[Primary colour]]s" can be mixed to make other colours. Red, green, and blue are the three traditional primary colours. The primary colours for television screens and computer monitors are red, green and blue. Printers and paints use magenta, yellow, and cyan as their primary colours; they may also use black. Sometimes this set of colours is simply called red, yellow, and blue.  People who can not see colours or have a distorted sense of colour are called [[colour blind]]. Most colour blind people are [[male]].  Colours are sometimes added to [[food]]. [[Food coloring|Food colouring]] is used to colour food, but some foods have natural colourings, like beta carotene.  When something has no colour, it is [[Transparency|transparent]]. An example is air.  The science of color is sometimes called chromatics, colorimetry, or simply color science.
