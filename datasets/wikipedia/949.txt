It is the capital of the [[States of Germany|state of]] [[Baden-Wrttemberg]] and of the [[Administrative District of Stuttgart]] (''Regierungsbezirk''). Stuttgart is on the river [[Neckar]]. In December 2011, 613,392 people live there.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.statistik.baden-wuerttemberg.de/Veroeffentl/Statistische_Berichte/3126_11001.pdf |title=Archived copy |access-date=2013-05-10 |archive-date=2013-05-04 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130504163001/http://www.statistik.baden-wuerttemberg.de/Veroeffentl/Statistische_Berichte/3126_11001.pdf |url-status=dead }}</ref>  Stuttgart is currently{{when}} the sixth largest German city and Stuttgart Region is the nation's third largest [[region]]. The city is divided into 23 city [[district]]s.  Stuttgart is the home of two [[car]] manufacturers: [[Mercedes Benz]] and [[Porsche]]. Porsche is in Zuffenhausen (North) and Mercedes in Untertrkheim (Southeast).  Stuttgart has an [[oceanic climate]] (''Cfb'' in the [[Koeppen climate classification]]).  == Population == Stuttgart has 613,392 people within city limits. It is the 6th largest city in Germany after [[Berlin]], [[Hamburg]], [[Munich]], [[Cologne]] and [[Frankfurt am Main]]. According to a 2005 estimate, the Stuttgart urban area had between 1,238,000 and 1,250,000 people.
