<title>Zaza language</title><text>{{Infobox language |name=Zaza |states=[[Anatolia]] |region=Main in [[Tunceli]], [[Bingl]], [[Erzincan]], [[Sivas]], [[Elaz]], [[Erzurum]], [[Malatya]] [[Gmhane Province]], [[anlurfa Province]], and [[Varto]], [[Adyaman Province]]; diasporic in [[Mutki]], [[Sarz]], [[Aksaray Province|Aksaray]], and [[Taraz]] |script=[[Latin script]] |ethnicity=[[Zazas|Zaza]] |speakers=1.6 million |date=1998 |ref=e18 |familycolor=Indo-European |fam2=[[Indo-Iranian languages|Indo-Iranian]] |fam3=[[Iranian languages|Iranian]] |fam4=[[Western Iranian languages|Western]] |fam5=[[Northwestern Iranian languages|Northwestern I]] |fam6=[[ZazaGorani languages|ZazaGorani]] |iso2=zza |lingua=58-AAA-ba |iso3=zza |lc1=kiu |ld1=[[Kirmanjki]] (Northern Zaza) |lc2=diq |ld2=[[Dimli]] (Southern Zaza) |glotto=zaza1246 |glottorefname=Zaza}} '''Zaza language''', also called '''Zazaki''', '''Kirmanjki''' and '''Dimli''', is an Indo-European language spoken in eastern [[Anatolia]] above the rivers [[Euphrates]] and [[Tigris]]. It belongs to the northwest-[[Iranian languages|Iranian]] group of Iranian language. The Zaza language is related to Sangsar, Mzandarn, Tt (Herzend), Semnn, Tlesh, [[Kurdish language|Kurdish]], [[Persian language|Persian]] and [[Balochi language|Balchi]].
