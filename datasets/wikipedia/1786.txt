<title>Void (astronomy)</title><text>[[File:Nearsc.gif|thumb|Nearby superclusters and voids]] In [[astronomy]], '''voids''' are the empty spaces between [[filament (astronomy)|filaments]]. Both filaments and Voids are one of the largest-scale [[structure]]s in the [[Universe]]. There are no or few [[galaxy|galaxies]] in voids. Most voids have a [[diameter]] of 11 to 150 [[parsec|Mpc]].  Especially large voids are the empty spaces without many [[supercluster]]s.  These voids are sometimes called '''supervoids'''.  A [[1994]] official counting ("''The structure of the Universe traced by rich clusters of galaxies.''", see References) lists a total of 27 supervoids with a distance of up to 740 Mpc.
